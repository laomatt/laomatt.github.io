Kitchen vs. Table learning

 

December 10, 2014

A table mentality in regards to learning is a very common way to think, and its how we are all programmed to think (well most of us) whether it be from parents, or teachers. On the other hand a kitchen mind set is more intuitive, experimental and most of all, its creative.

It’s important to look at devbootcamp as an opportunity. Not a program to go though. I know thats a little strange to say about a program and teaches … well.. programming, but hear me out.

We have to break away from the usual idea of education, that tells us that as long as we do good on a test, we’re educated, which is simply not true. Thats a lazy way of learning and an extremely lazy way of teaching. Students become afraid to experiment and learn on their own to the point that they can’t open a soda bottle without someone telling them to. I only say that because I remember an incident in kindergarten where I actually asked for permission to open a juice box while at lunch!

As a society we’ve been hypnotized into ‘drone’ learning, that does nothing but kill education and weakens our future economy.

A program like this is perfect for my learning style. Most of what I know about anything are things I found out by doing things. For example when I was learning how to draw and paint, I really sucked at first, I realized that I could only listen to so many lectures on the subject, but without all the practice and hours I put into it I wouldn’t be anywhere near as comfortable and proficient with the craft as I am today. The very fact that I can say that about myself with out feeling a little pretentious is evidence of all the hours I put into learning it. (Because you can’t work that hard on something and not call yourself proficient)

As someone who learns very little from lectures, and learns the most from practice and doing, I wouldn’t know half the things I know without all the man hours spent in front of my drawing table experimenting with different digital painting techniques, mixed media techniques, etc.. and I only hope I can bring that same philosophy to this program.

Personally I don’t care about grades or scores, or even diplomas, all I care about is what I can do and create after a program has run its course. I’m a creative person with a flair for logic and mathematics. I will always consider myself an artist first. Creativity and innovation is the most important thing for me to learn and cultivate.

I would say that I am pretty nervous though. This is a huge financial gamble for me, but I make up for that in my wanting of more knowledge and more ways to create things that are of use to people. Hopefully I can translate hunger for a job into real creativity when it comes to my web apps.

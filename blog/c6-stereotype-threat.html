    • Stereotype threat

    This reminds me of a Chappelle show skit,

    It can be crippling to have something like that continually hanging over your head. Unlike whats covered in the skit, I believe positive stereotypes to be just as crippling as negative ones.

    My own fears about it

    I fear the positive stereotypes just as much as the negative stereotypes. I get no kind of stereotype boost out of it. I fear that people want to expect more out of me, and have this entire back story assumed about me, prior to ever meeting me.

    I fear that people compare me to other Asians and Pacific islanders, which makes it really hard finding a job because of racial and gender quotas and affirmative action. I get no kind of lift or boost from any of those stereotype because they have never been me. I never grew up with a "tiger mom" (I fucking HATE that term), my mom was a single working mom, and I was a latch key child growing up in the Bayview district. Everything I was ever good at I got because I spent the hours and energy to learn it, while others around me consistently made the wrong choices. I went through the same schools, and failed the same placement tests in college, had to take remedial math because of the quality of education I received in an inner city school. But I dug my way out of it by working hard on my own, eventually graduating with a degree in math.

    All of a sudden that gets taken away from me because I happen to have an Asian last name. People who don't know any better think I have the "smart-gene". People I've met usually had their own interpretations about me and my life, and assumed I was an overachiever, and unlike other stereotypes, they were never shy about voicing their views. I think 'positive' stereotypes like this are just as damaging a negative stereotypes. When an individual gets judged based on how people perceive the group they belong to. This type of stereotyping is EXTREMELY harmful to a job search.

    I see stories about media hogs like Jesse Jackson and Al Charlatan (I know thats not his last name, I'm making a point) complaining that the tech industry doesn't have enough people of color, I can't help but to know that when they says people of color, while I agree that the tech industry needs to be more diverse, both gender wise as well as culture wise, what we should be focusing on is exposing inner city kids of all cultural backgrounds to computer language earlier. We are approaching a society in which the Internet and knowing how to navigate a computer is every bit as important as reading a book.
